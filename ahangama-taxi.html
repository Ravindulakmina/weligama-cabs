Ahangama taxi service

Ahangama, on the meter we don't use.

Ahangama has become a surf town in its own right — five world-class points within a few kilometres, a café scene that punches above its weight, and accommodation scattered along a 4 km strip of small lanes. Getting around without a private ride is doable on a tuk-tuk, but if you're carrying boards, a private vehicle is just simpler. That's where we come in.

FAQ

Ahangama taxi questions, answered.

Can I bring my surfboard in a sedan?

Yes — we put soft racks on every car for surf-town pickups. Up to two boards (under 7 ft) fit on a sedan; for longboards or three+ boards we recommend the SUV or van.

Is there a surcharge for boards?

No. As long as you tell us at booking, the racks and tie-downs are free. Loose-board damage is on you, fitted-rack transit is on us.

How do surf shuttles work?

Book a half-day ($45) and we'll drive you between Kabalana, Plantation, Insight, and Kathaluwa as the wind shifts. The driver waits while you surf and reloads boards between stops.

When is the surf season here?

November to April is the main southern season — clean offshore winds in the mornings, knee-to-head-high swell most days. May–October is the south coast's monsoon: still surfable, but messier.
