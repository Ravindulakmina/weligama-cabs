Hiriketiya taxi service

Hiriketiya, on the meter we don't use.

Hiriketiya — "Hiri" to regulars — is a perfect horseshoe bay tucked behind a coconut grove south of Tangalle. It punches massively above its weight: world-class yoga shalas, three or four genuinely excellent cafés, beginner-friendly surf, and a vibe that hasn't (yet) been overrun. The catch is getting there. The roads are narrow, signage is minimal, and most ride-sharing apps don't work this far south. That's where we come in.

  • 45 km from WeligamaRoughly 1 hr door-to-door, traffic depending.
  • Drivers who actually live thereThey know the shortcuts, the good restaurants, and the surf forecast.

FAQ

Hiriketiya taxi questions, answered.

Why is Hiriketiya more expensive than other south-coast rides?

It's further from our Weligama base — 45 km versus 8–15 km for Mirissa, Ahangama, or Matara. The fare reflects the longer round-trip distance and the slower last 5 km on narrow lanes.

Can you do a Hiri ↔ Weligama day trip?

Yes — $80 round-trip in a sedan, with up to 8 hours of wait time included. Plenty for a beach day plus lunch.

Do you do airport transfers to Mattala (HRI) instead of CMB?

Yes — Mattala is much closer (about 75 min from Hiri). Fixed at $55 in a sedan.

Is there parking at Hiriketiya bay?

It's tight — the main road dead-ends at the beach. Your driver will drop you at the closest viable spot (usually 30 m from the sand) and either wait nearby or return at an agreed time.
